Bus·an

부산 · 釜山 · プサン

A port city where mountains meet ocean, where seafood is religion and dialect is a badge of pride. Busan moves slower than Seoul, smiles wider, and rewards travelers who venture beyond the capital with beaches, temples on cliffs, and the country's freshest sashimi.

— 01 / What Makes Busan

Three things you'll fall for.

The qualities that make Busan a perfect contrast to Seoul — relaxed where Seoul is hurried, salty where Seoul is sleek.
01

Mountains, sea, and city — all in one frame

Few cities offer this geography. From a beach café you can see forested ridges. From a mountain temple you can see container ships at port. Busan's skyline isn't built around itself — it's built around the water.

02

Korea's most distinctive food culture

If Seoul does refinement, Busan does abundance. Live octopus at Jagalchi. Pork soup that simmers for 12 hours. Wheat noodles unique to this region. The seafood is so fresh it's still moving — and it's everywhere.

03

A city that doesn't perform for tourists

Locals speak satoori (Busan dialect) loudly and unapologetically. Shopkeepers bargain. Old men play go on the sidewalk. Even Haeundae Beach feels lived-in rather than curated. The authenticity is the attraction.

Korea's most famous beach.

A 1.5km crescent of golden sand backed by skyscrapers. Crowded in summer, peaceful in shoulder seasons. Premium hotels, seafood restaurants, and a coastal walking trail that leads to dramatic cliffs and a 200-year-old lighthouse.

Busan's original downtown.

★ Art & Culture

A village built on a hillside.

감천문화마을 · Korea's "Machu Picchu"

In the 1950s, Korean War refugees settled this steep hillside, building tiny pastel-colored houses. In 2009, the city turned it into an art village — adding murals, galleries, and walking trails. Today it's one of Busan's most photographed places, but real residents still live here.

— 04 / Suggested Itinerary

2 perfect days in Busan.

Most travelers come from Seoul for a weekend — these two days hit the essentials with breathing room.

Day One — The Postcard Day

하루 일정 · Gamcheon, Nampo, Gwangalli
9:30 AM
Take bus to Gamcheon Culture Village
감천문화마을 · From Toseong Stn (Line 1)

Take Line 1 to Toseong Station, then bus 2-2 up the mountain. Buy a stamp tour map at the visitor center.

10:30 AM
Wander Gamcheon's pastel alleys
감천 골목길 · Self-guided exploration

Don't rush. Climb to the lookout, photograph the Little Prince, browse galleries. Real people live here, so be respectful.

12:30 PM
Lunch back in Nampo
남포동 식당 · Korean BBQ or jokbal

Take a taxi back down (₩6k). Try jokbal (braised pork hocks) at any local spot.

2:00 PM
Walk Jagalchi & BIFF Square
자갈치시장·BIFF광장

Even if you don't eat seafood, walking through the market is unforgettable. BIFF Square is 5 min away.

4:00 PM
Sunset at Yongdusan Park
용두산공원 · Busan Tower

A short walk uphill. Tower entry ₩12k, but the park itself is free and has great views at golden hour.

7:00 PM
Dinner at Gwangalli with bridge view
광안리 회센터 · Sashimi by the sea

Take subway to Gwangan Stn. Pick a beach-front sashimi restaurant on the second floor for the bridge view.

9:00 PM
Beach walk under the lit bridge
광안리해수욕장 야경

The bridge lights rotate colors from 7pm. Walk the promenade, grab craft beer at any beachfront bar.

Day Two — Beach & City

바다 일정 · Haeundae and Seomyeon
8:00 AM
Sunrise at Haeundae Beach
해운대 일출

Worth the early wake-up. Korean tradition to watch sunrise here. Coffee shops nearby open at 7am.

10:00 AM
Walk Dongbaek Island loop
동백섬 · 1-hour coastal walk

Pine forest, mermaid statue, lighthouse, and APEC house. The whole loop takes about an hour with stops.

12:00 PM
Lunch — Haeundae market
해운대시장 · Local food

Try ssiat hotteok, mil myeon (Busan-style noodles), or any sashimi stall. Cash recommended.

2:00 PM
Take subway to Seomyeon
서면역 · Line 2

About 30 minutes. Browse Seomyeon Underground Mall — it's massive and bargain-friendly.

4:00 PM
Coffee at Jeonpo Café Street
전포카페거리 · Indie coffee

5-min walk from Seomyeon Stn. Each café has its own vibe — pick one that draws you in.

6:30 PM
Dwaeji gukbap dinner
서면 돼지국밥 골목 · Pork soup alley

Busan's signature dish — pork soup served with rice. Try Sinchang Gukbap or Yeongnam Sigol.

8:30 PM
Hwangnyeongsan night view
황령산 야경 · Night city panorama

Take a taxi (₩10k) up to the summit. The view of Busan at night is one of Korea's best — and free.

— 05 / Busan Food

Six dishes that define Busan.

Each one is unique to this city — you won't find them done as well anywhere else in Korea.
◉ Soup
Dwaeji Gukbap
돼지국밥 · Pork & rice soup

Pork bones simmered for 12+ hours into a rich, milky broth, served with rice and pork slices. Customize with chives, salted shrimp, and red pepper paste. Soul food during Korean War, now Busan's most defining dish.

Best at Sinchang Gukbap (신창국밥), Seomyeon
◉ Noodles
Mil Myeon
밀면 · Wheat cold noodles

Created by North Korean refugees who couldn't find buckwheat — they used wheat instead. The result is chewier than naengmyeon, served in icy beef broth. Uniquely Busan.

Best at Gaegeum Halmae Mil Myeon
◉ Seafood
Hwe (Sashimi)
회 · Korean-style sashimi

Cut thicker than Japanese sashimi, eaten with chogochujang (vinegar red pepper paste) wrapped in lettuce or sesame leaves. Pick your fish live at the market.

Best at Jagalchi Market 2nd floor
◉ Street Food
Ssiat Hotteok
씨앗호떡 · Seed-stuffed pancake

A Busan twist on the classic Korean street pancake — fried, then split open and stuffed with sunflower seeds, peanuts, and cinnamon. Crunchy, sweet, addictive.

Best at BIFF Square Hotteok stalls
◉ Fish Stew
Maeuntang
매운탕 · Spicy fish stew

Made with the fish bones and head left after sashimi, simmered with tofu, mushrooms, and red pepper. The deepest flavor in Korean seafood cooking. Order after eating sashimi.

Free with most sashimi orders at fish markets
◉ Late Night
Bibim Dangmyeon
비빔당면 · Cold glass noodles

Sweet potato glass noodles tossed with red pepper sauce, vegetables, and sometimes pig's blood sausage. Cheap, addictive, deeply Korean. Sold mostly in markets.

Best at Gukje Market food alley
— 06 / Busan-Specific Tips

Things only Busan locals know.

Practical advice from regulars and seasoned travelers — what to do, what to skip.
!
KTX is the only way from Seoul

2.5 hours, ₩60k one-way. Book through Korail or LetsKorail.com. Buses are cheaper but take 5 hours.

★
Subway covers most attractions

5 lines, color-coded. Get a T-Money card. Gamcheon and Hwangnyeongsan need bus or taxi.

✓
Avoid August beach crowds

Korean summer vacation makes Haeundae unwalkable. May–June or September are perfect.

◉
Sunrise at Haeundae is real

Korean New Year tradition — locals come at 5am. Even on regular days, the sunrise is genuinely worth waking up for.

!
Cash for markets

Jagalchi, Gukje, Bupyeong markets prefer cash. Most other places accept cards — Korea is mostly cashless.

★
BIFF in October

Busan International Film Festival, early October. Even non-cinephiles will love the energy. Book accommodation 2 months ahead.

✓
Satoori is a charm, not a barrier

Busan dialect is famously different. Locals will switch to standard Korean for visitors, but their satoori is part of the experience.

◉
Day trip to Tongyeong

1.5 hours by bus. Often called "Naples of Korea" — small fishing port with gorgeous islands. Great if you have an extra day.
