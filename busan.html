Bus·an

부산 · 釜山 · プサン

A port city where mountains meet ocean, where seafood is religion and dialect is a badge of pride. Busan moves slower than Seoul, smiles wider, and rewards travelers who venture beyond the capital with beaches, temples on cliffs, and the country's freshest sashimi.

— 01 / What Makes Busan

Three things you'll fall for.

The qualities that make Busan a perfect contrast to Seoul — relaxed where Seoul is hurried, salty where Seoul is sleek.
01

Mountains, sea, and city — all in one frame

Few cities offer this geography. From a beach café you can see forested ridges. From a mountain temple you can see container ships at port. Busan's skyline isn't built around itself — it's built around the water.

02

Korea's most distinctive food culture

If Seoul does refinement, Busan does abundance. Live octopus at Jagalchi. Pork soup that simmers for 12 hours. Wheat noodles unique to this region. The seafood is so fresh it's still moving — and it's everywhere.

03

A city that doesn't perform for tourists

Locals speak satoori (Busan dialect) loudly and unapologetically. Shopkeepers bargain. Old men play go on the sidewalk. Even Haeundae Beach feels lived-in rather than curated. The authenticity is the attraction.

Haeundae
해운대 · The Beach Capital
Korea's Most Famous Beach
★ Beach & Resort

Korea's most famous beach.

해운대 · The crown jewel of Busan

A 1.5km crescent of golden sand backed by skyscrapers. Crowded in summer, peaceful in shoulder seasons. Premium hotels, seafood restaurants, and a coastal walking trail that leads to dramatic cliffs and a 200-year-old lighthouse. The beach itself is just the starting point — explore the connected Dongbaek Island and Mipo coastal trail for the full experience.

A 1.5km crescent of fine golden sand with the city skyline as backdrop. Korean New Year sunrise tradition draws thousands at 5am every January 1st. Even in regular days, sunrise here is iconic — locals come early to watch with coffee. Free, open 24/7.

서면 · Where two subway lines cross

The commercial center of Busan — and where Lines 1 and 2 intersect. Underground shopping arcades stretch for blocks. Late-night food alleys come alive after 10pm. Nampo
남포동 · The Old Port Town
Markets, Cinema & Korean History
★ Old Town

Busan's original downtown.

남포동 · The historic port heart

Before Haeundae was developed, this was Busan. The port, the markets, the cinema district — all here. Today Nampo is a walkable old-town with the country's largest fish market, an iconic cinema street where the Busan International Film Festival started, and Yongdusan Park's famous tower. It's grittier and more authentic than Haeundae, with deeper history.

A 40-step staircase that became a meeting point for separated families during the Korean War. Bronze statues recreate iconic scenes. Free outdoor museum surrounding the stairs. 5-min walk from Jungang Station. Quiet historical break from busy markets.

Gamcheon
감천문화마을 · The Art Village
Korea's Machu Picchu · Pastel Hillside
★ Art & Culture

A village built on a hillside.

감천문화마을 · Korea's "Machu Picchu"

In the 1950s, Korean War refugees settled this steep hillside, building tiny pastel-colored houses stacked one above another. In 2009, the city turned it into an art village — adding murals, galleries, walking trails, and small museums. Today it's one of Busan's most photographed places, but real residents still live here. Walk slowly, speak quietly.

Gwangalli

Sunset · Drone Shows · Locals' Favorite
★ Beach + Bridge

The second beach, with the better view.

광안리 · Locals' favorite beach

Less crowded than Haeundae, but with the iconic Gwangan Bridge as backdrop. The bridge lights up at night with rotating colors and hosts monthly drone shows that draw thousands. The promenade has a younger, hipper vibe — surf shops, craft beer bars, and design-forward cafés. Most locals prefer this beach to Haeundae. The energy is real and unforced.

— 04 / Suggested Itinerary

2 perfect days in Busan.

Most travelers come from Seoul for a weekend — these two days hit the essentials with breathing room.

Day One — The Postcard Day

— 05 / Busan Food

Six dishes that define Busan.

Each one is unique to this city — you won't find them done as well anywhere else in Korea.
◉ Soup
Dwaeji Gukbap
돼지국밥 · Pork & rice soup

Pork bones simmered for 12+ hours into a rich, milky broth, served with rice and tender pork slices. Customize at the table with chives, salted shrimp, and red pepper paste. Soul food during the Korean War, now Busan's most defining dish. Eaten any time of day.

◉ Noodles
Mil Myeon
밀면 · Wheat cold noodles

Created by North Korean refugees who couldn't find buckwheat — they used wheat instead. The result is chewier than naengmyeon, served in icy beef broth or with spicy bibim sauce. Uniquely Busan, available in two styles: mul-milmyeon (cold soup) and bibim-milmyeon (spicy mixed).

◉ Seafood
Hwe (Sashimi)
회 · Korean-style sashimi

Cut thicker than Japanese sashimi, eaten with chogochujang (vinegar red pepper paste) wrapped in lettuce or sesame leaves with garlic and pepper. Pick your fish live from the tank — flounder, sea bream, halibut. The freshness is the point.

◉ Street Food
Ssiat Hotteok
씨앗호떡 · Seed-stuffed pancake

A Busan twist on the classic Korean street pancake — fried until crispy, then split open and stuffed with sunflower seeds, peanuts, brown sugar, and cinnamon. The seeds add crunch that the original lacks. Best eaten standing on the street, hot from the griddle.

◉ Fish Stew
Maeuntang
매운탕 · Spicy fish stew

Made with the fish bones, head, and tail left after sashimi, simmered with tofu, mushrooms, scallions, and red pepper paste. The deepest flavor in Korean seafood cooking. Most sashimi restaurants offer this as a follow-up dish — never order it alone.

— 06 / Busan-Specific Tips

Things only Busan locals know.

Practical advice from regulars and seasoned travelers — what to do, what to skip.
!
KTX is the only way from Seoul

2.5 hours, ₩60k one-way. Book through Korail or LetsKorail.com. Buses are cheaper but take 5 hours.

★
Subway covers most attractions

5 lines, color-coded. Get a T-Money card. Gamcheon and Hwangnyeongsan need bus or taxi.

✓
Avoid August beach crowds

Korean summer vacation makes Haeundae unwalkable. May–June or September are perfect.

◉
Sunrise at Haeundae is real

Korean New Year tradition — locals come at 5am. Even on regular days, the sunrise is genuinely worth waking up for.

!
Cash for markets

Jagalchi, Gukje, Bupyeong markets prefer cash. Most other places accept cards — Korea is mostly cashless.

★
BIFF in October

Busan International Film Festival, early October. Even non-cinephiles will love the energy. Book accommodation 2 months ahead.

✓
Satoori is a charm, not a barrier

Busan dialect is famously different. Locals will switch to standard Korean for visitors, but their satoori is part of the experience.

◉
Day trip to Tongyeong

1.5 hours by bus. Often called "Naples of Korea" — small fishing port with gorgeous islands. Great if you have an extra day.
