Je·ju

제주도 · 濟州島 · チェジュ島

A volcanic island shaped by an eruption two million years ago, Jeju is Korea's Hawaii — but with hanok villages, Buddhist temples carved into cliffs, and lava tubes you can walk through. The pace is slower, the air is salty, and somehow every road ends at a view that takes your breath away.

— 01 / What Makes Jeju

Three things you'll never find on the mainland.

Jeju isn't just another Korean destination — it's a different culture, climate, and landscape, all packed into a 73km-wide island.
01

A volcanic island, geologically alien

Lava tubes you can walk through. Black sand beaches next to white. A 1,950-meter volcano at the center. Hexagonal columns of basalt rising from the sea. Jeju formed in eruptions and never lost the drama.

02

A culture older than mainland Korea

Until 1948, Jeju was its own kingdom. The dialect is so distinct mainland Koreans need subtitles. The haenyeo — female free divers in their 70s and 80s — still harvest seafood the way their grandmothers did.

03

A pace that forces you to slow down

No rush hour. No crowded subways. Even peak Jeju moves at half-Seoul speed. The island has 26 walking trails (the famous Olle), endless coastal cafés, and locals who genuinely have time to chat.

Seongsan Sunrise Peak
성산일출봉 · UNESCO World Heritage

A 180m volcanic crater that erupted from the seabed 5,000 years ago, now standing as Jeju's most iconic landmark. The 30-minute hike to the rim rewards with panoramic views of the crater lake, sea cliffs, and (at sunrise) one of the world's most beautiful natural phenomena. Strenuous but accessible to most.

Manjanggul Lava Tube
만장굴 · World's longest lava tube

A 13km lava tube formed 250,000 years ago, with 1km open to the public. Walk through ancient volcanic underground — see massive lava columns, lava stalactites, and the world's largest lava column at the end. Stays a constant 15°C year-round, so bring a jacket even in summer. Surreal experience.

Woljeongri Beach

Jeju's most photographed beach — turquoise water meeting white sand, with rows of design-forward cafés along the coast. Each café has a distinct concept (industrial, surfer, minimalist). Order a coffee, sit on the terrace, watch the waves. Less crowded on weekday mornings. The water here is genuinely Caribbean-clear.

Haenyeo Museum

The haenyeo are Jeju's UNESCO-listed female free-divers — women in their 60s-80s who still harvest seafood from the seabed without scuba gear. This museum tells their story with photos, dive equipment, and videos. Real haenyeo perform diving demonstrations daily at Sumbisori Park nearby. Inspiring and quintessentially Jeju.

Hyeopjae Beach

Often considered Jeju's most beautiful beach. White sand, turquoise water, with the small Biyangdo Island visible offshore. Calmer and shallower than Woljeongri, making it more swimmable. Multiple beach cafés. Camping allowed in the adjacent pine forest park. About 45 minutes from Jeju City.

South
서귀포 · Resort & Waterfalls

The luxe side of Jeju.

서귀포 · Resort capital

Jeju's southern coast is warmer, lusher, and home to the island's resort hotels and three famous waterfalls. The temperature here stays mild even in winter (rarely below 10°C). Tangerine groves cover the rolling hills. Most all-inclusive Jeju trips are based here for good reason — the convenience, the climate, and the dramatic coastal scenery all align. Slightly more touristy than the east, but no less beautiful.

Perfect basalt columns formed when ancient lava cooled at exactly the right rate, creating natural hexagonal pillars. The walking deck stretches along the cliff edge with crashing waves below. Center
한라산 · Volcano & Forests
Korea's Highest Peak · Hiking Trails
★ Volcano & forests

The volcano at the heart.

한라산 · The center of the island

Hallasan rises 1,950 meters from sea level — South Korea's highest peak and the dormant volcano that built Jeju Island. For many travelers, climbing it isn't optional; it's a pilgrimage. Around the mountain are forest trails, oreum (mini-volcanos you can hike in 30 minutes), and surprisingly accessible day-hike options. Even non-hikers should drive through the mid-mountain zone for the dramatic landscapes.

— 05 / Jeju Food

Six things you can only eat here.

Jeju's cuisine is unique to the island — formed by black volcanic soil, abundant seafood, and isolation from the mainland.
◉ BBQ
Heuk-dwaeji
흑돼지 · Jeju black pork

A native Jeju pig breed with darker meat and richer fat than mainland pork. Grilled at your table over charcoal, then wrapped in lettuce with garlic and the local "myeolchi-jeot" (anchovy fish sauce) instead of regular ssamjang. The most iconic Jeju dish — every traveler tries it at least once.

◉ Fish
Okdom-gui
옥돔구이 · Grilled tilefish

A small, premium fish only caught in the deep waters off Jeju. Salt-grilled until crispy outside, tender inside. A staple at Jeju traditional restaurants and special-occasion meals. The fish has a distinctive sweet, almost buttery flavor. Served whole — eat with chopsticks, picking carefully around small bones.

◉ Citrus
Hallabong
한라봉 · Jeju's premium tangerine

A hybrid orange named after Hallasan mountain, with a distinctive bump on top resembling the volcano's peak. Sweet, juicy, with thin skin that peels easily. Fresh from December to March; products (juice, jam, chocolate, tea) all year. Often more expensive than regular oranges but in a different league flavor-wise.

◉ Noodles
Gogi Guksu
고기국수 · Pork noodle soup

Wheat noodles in pork bone broth, topped with sliced pork, scallions, and pepper. Cheap (~₩7-9k), filling, and uniquely Jeju. Said to have originated from local fishermen who needed a hearty meal. Different from mainland Korea's pork bone soups — the broth is lighter and the noodles thicker.

◉ Sashimi
Hwe (Jeju Sashimi)
회 · Jeju-style raw fish

Cut from fish caught hours before serving — the freshness is the point. Locals eat it with chogochujang (vinegar red pepper paste) wrapped in lettuce or sesame leaves with rice. Different from Japanese sashimi: cut thicker, dipped in spicy sauce, eaten as a wrap. Order sashimi sets that include side dishes and ending soup.

— 06 / Jeju-Specific Tips

What every first-timer should know.

Jeju runs differently from mainland Korea. These tips save days of wandering and dollars wasted.
!
Rent a car — really

Public buses exist but are slow and limited. International driving permit (IDP) accepted. Rentals from ₩40k/day. Roads are easy.

★
Book Hallasan trails 1 month ahead

Summit trails (Seongpanak, Gwaneumsa) require online reservation at visitjeju.net. Free, but limited daily slots.

✓
Avoid July and August typhoons

Late summer brings heavy rain and typhoons. Activities cancel often. April-June and September-October are perfect.

◉
Cheap flights from Seoul daily

30+ flights per day from Gimpo (1hr) and Incheon (1.5hr). Book early for ₩50k tickets. Last minute can hit ₩200k.

!
Stay in different zones

Jeju's east and west are 1.5hr apart by car. Many travelers split — 2 nights east, 1 night west. Avoids long drives daily.

★
Tangerine farms in winter

Dec–Feb you can pick hallabong straight from trees. Many farms charge ~₩10k for all-you-can-eat experience.

✓
Olle Trail walking

26 numbered routes around the island, total 425km. Pick any 1-day section by number. Color-coded ribbons mark the path.

◉
Jeju dialect is real

Older locals may speak Jeju-mal which differs from Korean. Younger people speak standard Korean and basic English.
