Je·ju

제주도 · 濟州島 · チェジュ島

A volcanic island shaped by an eruption two million years ago, Jeju is Korea's Hawaii — but with hanok villages, Buddhist temples carved into cliffs, and lava tubes you can walk through. The pace is slower, the air is salty, and somehow every road ends at a view that takes your breath away.

— 01 / What Makes Jeju

Three things you'll never find on the mainland.

Jeju isn't just another Korean destination — it's a different culture, climate, and landscape, all packed into a 73km-wide island.
01

A volcanic island, geologically alien

Lava tubes you can walk through. Black sand beaches next to white. A 1,950-meter volcano at the center. Hexagonal columns of basalt rising from the sea. Jeju formed in eruptions and never lost the drama.

02

A culture older than mainland Korea

Until 1948, Jeju was its own kingdom. The dialect is so distinct mainland Koreans need subtitles. The haenyeo — female free divers in their 70s and 80s — still harvest seafood the way their grandmothers did.

03

A pace that forces you to slow down

No rush hour. No crowded subways. Even peak Jeju moves at half-Seoul speed. The island has 26 walking trails (the famous Olle), endless coastal cafés, and locals who genuinely have time to chat.

The luxe side of Jeju.

The quieter coast.

★ Volcano & forests

The volcano at the heart.

한라산 · The center of the island

Hallasan rises 1,950 meters from sea level — South Korea's highest peak and the dormant volcano that built the island. Climbing isn't optional for many travelers; it's a pilgrimage. Around the mountain are forest trails, oreum (mini-volcanos), and surprisingly accessible day-hike options.

— 04 / Suggested Itinerary

3 perfect days in Jeju.

A balanced loop hitting east, south, and west. Renting a car is essential — public transport on Jeju is limited.

Day One — The East Side

동부 일정 · Sunrise Peak, lava tubes, beach cafés
5:30 AM
Sunrise at Seongsan Peak
성산일출봉 · A 30-min hike

Early start, but unforgettable. Hike up before sunrise — the view from the rim looking east is iconic. ₩5k entry.

9:00 AM
Breakfast at Seongsan port
성산항 · Seafood breakfast

Fresh haemul jeon (seafood pancake) or transparent fish soup. Restaurants open 7am for fishermen.

11:00 AM
Manjanggul Lava Tube
만장굴 · World's longest lava tube

Walk a kilometer through ancient lava cave. Cool inside (15°C year-round), bring a light jacket. ₩4k.

1:00 PM
Lunch & beach time at Woljeongri
월정리 해변 · Beach café row

One of Jeju's prettiest beaches, lined with stylish cafés. Pick a beach café for lunch with ocean view.

3:30 PM
Bijarim Forest walk
비자림 · 800-year old trees

Easy 1-hour walk under ancient nutmeg trees. Cooler than the coast, fragrant, calming.

6:00 PM
Dinner at Gimnyeong Village
김녕마을 · Local seafood

Try jeonbok-juk (abalone porridge) or okdom-gui (grilled tilefish) — Jeju specialties. Cash sometimes preferred.

Day Two — South & Center

서귀포·한라산 · Waterfalls and a forest hike
9:00 AM
Drive south, stop at Saryeoni Forest
사려니숲길 · Cedar forest walk

Cross-island drive takes 1hr, with stops. Walk a section of the 15km cedar forest path — fresh air after city.

11:30 AM
Cheonjiyeon Waterfall
천지연폭포 · 22m waterfall

Easy 10-min walk from parking. Photogenic from any angle. ₩2k entry.

12:30 PM
Lunch at Olle Market
서귀포 매일올레시장 · Local lunch

Try heuk-dwaeji (black pork) at any sit-down restaurant near the market. ₩30–40k for two with sides.

2:30 PM
Jeongbang Waterfall
정방폭포 · Falls into the sea

Asia's only waterfall that drops directly into the ocean. Wear shoes you don't mind getting wet.

4:00 PM
Jusangjeolli Cliff
주상절리 · Hexagonal lava columns

Walking deck along basalt cliffs. Surreal geological formation. Sunset here is incredible.

6:30 PM
Dinner in Jungmun
중문 · Resort area dining

Many waterfront restaurants here. Try haemultang (seafood stew) for a special meal. ₩50k for two.

Day Three — The Peaceful West

서부 일정 · Tea fields and sunset
10:00 AM
Hyeopjae Beach morning
협재해수욕장 · White sand & turquoise

Walk to neighboring Geumneung Beach (15 min). Crystal-clear water, very few crowds in morning.

12:00 PM
Lunch at a beach café
협재 카페거리 · Beachside dining

Many design-forward cafés along the coast. Try Bomnal Café or Cafe AGOK for ocean views.

1:30 PM
O'sulloc Tea Fields
오설록 티뮤지엄 · Green tea farm

Free museum, endless tea fields for photos, and the famous green tea ice cream. Spend 1.5 hours.

3:30 PM
Sanbangsan & Yongmeori Coast
산방산·용머리해안

Climb halfway up Sanbangsan for sea views. The coastal trail below is worth the walk.

5:30 PM
Sunset at Hangmori cliff trail
송악산 둘레길 · Volcanic cliff walk

The west's best sunset spot. Easy 1-hour cliff walk, looking out over Marado Island.

7:30 PM
Final dinner — black pork BBQ
제주 흑돼지 · Heuk-dwaeji

Don't leave Jeju without this. Black-pig pork belly grilled at your table — fattier and more flavorful than mainland pork.

— 05 / Jeju Food

Six things you can only eat here.

Jeju's cuisine is unique to the island — formed by black volcanic soil, abundant seafood, and isolation from the mainland.
◉ BBQ
Heuk-dwaeji
흑돼지 · Jeju black pork

A native Jeju pig breed with darker meat and richer fat. Grilled at your table, wrapped in lettuce with garlic and ssamjang. The island's most iconic dish.

Best at 흑돼지거리 (Black Pig Street), Jeju City
◉ Seafood
Jeonbok-juk
전복죽 · Abalone porridge

Made from abalones harvested by haenyeo (female free divers), simmered slowly with rice into a creamy green-tinted porridge. Comforting and luxurious at once.

Best at Olle Market or any haenyeo restaurant
◉ Fish
Okdom-gui
옥돔구이 · Grilled tilefish

A small, premium fish only caught off Jeju. Salt-grilled until crispy outside, tender inside. A staple at Jeju traditional restaurants and breakfast tables.

Best in Seogwipo coastal restaurants
◉ Citrus
Hallabong
한라봉 · Jeju's premium orange

A hybrid orange named after Hallasan mountain — sweet, juicy, with a distinctive bump on top. Fresh from December–March, but products (juice, jam, chocolate) all year.

Buy at any market or fruit stand
◉ Noodles
Gogi Guksu
고기국수 · Pork noodle soup

Wheat noodles in pork bone broth, topped with sliced pork, scallions, and pepper. Cheap (~₩7k), filling, and uniquely Jeju.

Best at 고기국수거리 (Noodle Street), Jeju City
◉ Sashimi
Hwe
회 · Jeju-style sashimi

Cut from fish caught hours before serving. Locals eat it with chogochujang or rolled in seaweed with rice. The freshness is the point.

Best at Seongsan or Aewol harbors
— 06 / Jeju-Specific Tips

What every first-timer should know.

Jeju runs differently from mainland Korea. These tips save days of wandering and dollars wasted.
!
Rent a car — really

Public buses exist but are slow and limited. International driving permit (IDP) accepted. Rentals from ₩40k/day. Roads are easy.

★
Book Hallasan trails 1 month ahead

Summit trails (Seongpanak, Gwaneumsa) require online reservation at visitjeju.net. Free, but limited daily slots.

✓
Avoid July and August typhoons

Late summer brings heavy rain and typhoons. Activities cancel often. April-June and September-October are perfect.

◉
Cheap flights from Seoul daily

30+ flights per day from Gimpo (1hr) and Incheon (1.5hr). Book early for ₩50k tickets. Last minute can hit ₩200k.

!
Stay in different zones

Jeju's east and west are 1.5hr apart by car. Many travelers split — 2 nights east, 1 night west. Avoids long drives daily.

★
Tangerine farms in winter

Dec–Feb you can pick hallabong straight from trees. Many farms charge ~₩10k for all-you-can-eat experience.

✓
Olle Trail walking

26 numbered routes around the island, total 425km. Pick any 1-day section by number. Color-coded ribbons mark the path.

◉
Jeju dialect is real

Older locals may speak Jeju-mal which differs from Korean. Younger people speak standard Korean and basic English.
